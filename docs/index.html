Computer vision and circuit simulation

Reading hand-drawn circuits and solving them

This is a project I built that takes a picture of a circuit drawn on paper, works out what the components are and how they connect, and then solves the circuit with a simulation engine I wrote myself. It also checks its own answers against SPICE and against the standard textbook formulas.

It starts from a photo of the drawing, finds the parts, traces the wires into connections, builds a netlist, and then solves for the node voltages and branch currents.

01How it works

There are five stages. Three of them are built and tested today. The component detector is the next thing I need to train, and the live overlay comes after that.

02The circuit solver

Everything sits on top of one linear solve, using companion models, so there is only one piece of math that actually has to be correct.

03Accuracy and results

An extraction only counts as correct when its netlist is the same circuit electrically as the known answer. That check is done with graph isomorphism.

04What it cannot do yet

These are the real limits at the moment, and I think it is worth writing them down plainly.

Things I am not claiming

  • No results on real photos yet. Everything here runs on synthetic images that I generate. Reading photos of my own handwriting needs the trained detector, which is the next phase, so I am not claiming that part works.
  • Speckle and uneven lighting break the current image cleanup. I measured this in the noise study. The fix is adaptive thresholding, which is planned but not built.
  • The ngspice cross check does not cover inductor circuits yet. The textbook formulas cover those cases for now.

05Reproducing the results
